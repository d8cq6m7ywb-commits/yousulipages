How 2026+ qualifying works

The system changed. Three pieces explain it.

01

AG winners auto-qualify

The fastest finisher in each age group + gender at a qualifier event gets one automatic slot. If they decline, the slot rolls down to 2nd, then 3rd. If still unclaimed, it moves into the performance pool — but only into the same-gender pool.

02

Gender-split performance pools

The remaining slots split into a men’s pool and women’s pool. The split mirrors the ratio of male-to-female starters at that race. Within each pool, slots go to the fastest age-graded times.

03

Age-graded ranking

Your raw finish time × the official Kona / 70.3 Standard ratio for your age + gender. Lower wins. The ratio is meant to put a fast 70-year-old and a fast 30-year-old on the same scale within their gender.

No course-difficulty penalty (unlike Boston). Ranking is relative to your event’s field, after age-grading, within your gender. This calculator is an unofficial helper — defer to IRONMAN’s published documentation and on-site roll-down lists.

Coach note

Pick the right race — not just the right time.

The 2026 system rewards three things, in this order:

  • Win your AG. Auto-qualifying slot is the cleanest path. Pick a race where the depth in your AG is shallow — smaller, less famous events have fewer fast competitors per gender.
  • Be top of your gender pool. If you don’t win AG, your age-graded time has to beat most of the gender pool. The number depends entirely on the gender breakdown of starters — in heavily-male races, female pool slots are scarce.
  • Decline rates roll down. Some AG winners decline (cost, family, injury). If you finished 2nd or 3rd in AG, you’re first in the roll-down queue. Show up at the slot allocation ceremony.

Practical: if you’re ~5–10 min short of an AG win, look at events with fewer entrants in your AG, smaller fields overall, harder bike courses (slower times = more spread = bigger margin matters less), or tougher conditions (heat, hills) that thin the field.

About the age-grade ratios

Where the numbers come from and what they mean.

Kona Standard (IM full)

Peak ~30–34 men, ~30–34 women

Built from historical age-group winners' relative times. Ratio = your AG time / the absolute peak (=1.00). Younger and older AGs get lower ratios; multiplying your time by your ratio brings them all to a comparable scale.

70.3 Standard

Different curve from full

Half-distance ratios are not the same as Kona ratios. Half-distance racing is more aerobic-power-limited; full-distance more durability-limited. The age-fade differs accordingly.

Within-gender

Men compete vs men, women vs women

The ratios are gender-specific. There’s no cross-gender ranking — the 2026 system puts each gender in its own pool with its own slots. Your age-graded time only matters relative to others of your gender at your event.
