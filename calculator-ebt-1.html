Yousuli verdict —

How DPS & SR work together

Speed = SR × DPS. To swim faster, raise one without dropping the other.

01

Distance per stroke (DPS)

How far each pull moves you. Bigger people can have bigger DPS — that’s why we anchor to height. DPS / height between 60% and 65% is the balanced range for most well-trained swimmers.

02

Stroke rate (SR)

How many pulls per minute. Faster swimming generally needs higher SR — but only if DPS doesn’t collapse. The Yousuli SR table sets acceptable SR ranges by pace bracket.

03

The verdict

If your SR is below the acceptable range for your pace → Increase SR. If above → Increase DPS (you’re thrashing). If inside → Balanced — you’re using the right combination for that speed.

Coach note

What to actually do with each verdict.

  • Increase SR — you’re gliding too long. Drills: tempo trainer at +3–5 SPM, fast-arms 25s with paddles, in/out 50s focused on quick hand entry. Don’t trade DPS for rate; aim for both.
  • Increase DPS — you’re thrashing. Drills: catch-up, fingertip drag, single-arm pull with breath control. Check your hand entry, body line, and high elbow catch. Stroke rate alone won’t fix swim speed.
  • Balanced — congrats; the next gain is at the same balance one band higher. Re-test after a 4–6 week training block to see if you’ve moved bands.

Re-test every 4–6 weeks at the same distance, same pool, same time of day. Single-time numbers are noisy; trends matter. Pair this with EBT-2 (tipping points) for a fuller picture — that test finds the speed where DPS collapses, which tells you exactly when to switch from glide to grind.

DPS rating bands

Where each band sits as a % of height — and what it means.

< 55%

Very low

You’re putting in many strokes for the distance. Almost certainly a technique fix — body position, catch, pull path. Drill-heavy block.

55–60%

Low

Improvable. DPS work pays. Most adult-onset swimmers sit here when they start. 4–8 weeks of focused technique typically moves you up a band.

60–65%

Balanced

Where most well-trained age-groupers live. From here, gains come from raising both SR and DPS together, not one in isolation.

65–70%

High

Strong technique, long pull. Watch for over-gliding — if SR is dropping below your pace bracket, you’re leaving speed on the table.

≥ 70%

Very high

Elite territory or extreme glide. If race pace is slow despite huge DPS, you’re under-using SR. Add tempo work.
