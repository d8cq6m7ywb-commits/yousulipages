My story

Some moments don’t define you, but they shape how you see the world.

01 · Little

The wall of the pool

It would be a mistake to tie your identity to one event — but some moments do change how you see the world.

When I was about six months old, I fell into a pool and was found about five minutes later. I survived by pushing off the bottom to breathe. For years, the wall of that pool was the only thing I dreamed about.

Later, I grew up extremely active and found my way into sports: swimming, skiing, riding, and combat sports.

02 · Journey

Freedom elsewhere

Perhaps because I didn’t feel protected from danger as a child, I learned early to look for freedom elsewhere — and built tools to cope with hardship and SA. I chased adventure and, when I could, escaped into the wilderness.

As a teenager I set out to travel, and those years shaped me more than any plan ever could — through good and bad experiences across India, China, Canada, and much of Europe.

03 · Coaching

From martial arts to endurance

After about ten years in martial arts — including two years training in a remote village in China — I began coaching MMA and self-defense in 2005 at BLCU (Beijing).

My love for combat sports never changed, but the move into endurance sport showed me something important: it can be both therapeutic and widely accessible. Since then, I’ve stayed on a simple mission: keep learning, so the people I coach waste less time and get better outcomes.

04 · Athlete

Long projects, mind & mechanics

I’ve always been more drawn to training than racing. I like long projects that test biomechanics and the limits of the mind. In 2015, I completed a marathon every week across China and was featured in a national documentary.

During the COVID years I took on the 7030+: 1.9 km swim, 90 km bike, 21 km run — repeated for 101 consecutive days. I’ve crossed seas in open-water swims (Italy to France, the smaller but mythical Galilee). And there will be more along the way.

Quick FAQ

Common questions before applying for coaching.

Do you coach remotely?

Yes. I’ve worked with athletes locally in greater Los Angeles and internationally. Day-to-day prescription, feedback, and adjustments work fully remotely; testing days at the Performance Lab or partner facilities pair in when useful.

Do you coach beginners?

Yes — from true beginners aiming to finish a first race, to age-groupers chasing championship qualification, to elites aiming for the win. The plan changes; the standard of attention doesn’t.

Triathlon vs. running-only?

I’m comfortable across swim, bike, and run mechanics. If the question is “can you coach running only?” — absolutely. Plenty of Boston and UTMB qualifiers in the back-catalog.

Do you travel for workshops?

Yes — I’m actively building workshops and training camps in Europe, Asia, and the Middle East. Send a note if you have a venue or community in mind.

Want to work together?

Send a quick note. Tell me about your sport, your level, your race goals over the next 12–18 months, and the constraints you’re training around. I reply within 1–2 business days.
