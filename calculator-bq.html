The acceptance reality

Hitting the BQ standard is necessary, not sufficient.

01

BQ-X cutoffs since 2014

Boston has been over-subscribed every year since 2014. That means the BAA accepts the fastest qualifiers in each age group, not all of them. Recent cutoffs have ranged from BQ −1:39 (2025 race) to BQ −5:29 (2024 race). Plan to be ~5–7 minutes under if you want a realistic shot.

02

2026 standards = 5 min tighter

The BAA tightened all standards by 5 minutes for the 2026 race onward. The intent: reduce the cutoff buffer needed. Early signs are it’s working, but until we have several cycles of data, treat BQ-5 as the realistic target.

03

Downhill rules (eff. 2027 reg.)

For races from Sep 13, 2025 onward, courses with net drop ≥ 1500 ft get a time penalty. 1500–2999 ft → +5:00 · 3000–5999 ft → +10:00 · ≥ 6000 ft → not eligible. Targets the “mountain marathons” (Tunnel, REVEL, Mt. Charleston) that flooded BQ entries with downhill-aided times.

Standards and downhill policy come from the Boston Athletic Association. Always verify on the BAA website before registering, rules can change between cycles.

Coach note

Train for BQ minus 7, race for BQ minus 5.

Three patterns in athletes who get to Boston:

  • They pick the right qualifying race. Flat, fast, certified, weather-stable, well-supported. Boston-area marathoners disproportionately qualify at California International (CIM), Indianapolis, Chicago, or Berlin, not at hill challenges.
  • They build a buffer year. Hit BQ in the spring qualifier, sit on it through the registration window, don’t race-bank-on a fall PR. Stress-free registration is worth the patience.
  • They train for the standard, not the buffer. If you’re shooting for BQ, train for BQ-7. If race-day execution gives you BQ-5, perfect. Aim too tight to the standard and a 1% bad day kills it.

For honest pacing targets at BQ effort: pair this with the Advanced running predictor to convert your recent 5K / 10K / half into a marathon target. And lab testing gives you the metabolic crossover so race-day fueling doesn’t become the limiter.
