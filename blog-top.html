What we write about

Six recurring topic clusters, the questions athletes actually ask in coaching calls and the lab.

Cluster 01 · Physiology

VO2, RMR, lactate, RER

What the numbers mean, how they’re measured, and how to read your own report without a coach in the room. RED-S front-doors. The oxygen cascade. Heat & altitude work. Kinetics.

Cluster 02 · Aero & equipment

CdA, Crr, fitting

Field-based CdA protocols, what really moves the needle on tri bikes, what doesn’t, and how to test your own setup. Tire pressure, rolling resistance, helmet choice, race-day setup.

Cluster 03 · Fueling

Race-day & intra-workout nutrition

Concrete plans athletes actually execute. Sweat-rate testing, sodium math, gut training, how Neversecond / Maurten / SiS / etc. compare in practice. Race-week glycogen logistics.

Cluster 04 · Technique

Swim, run, & bike biomechanics

Stroke mechanics that actually translate to open water. Run-form fixes that survive fatigue. Pedal-stroke economy. Specific cues you can use when the coach isn’t there.

Cluster 05 · Athlete stories

Coached athlete case studies

What we tested, what we changed, what happened. Real numbers, real timelines. The good blocks and the ones that didn’t go to plan. Sub-3 marathon, first IRONMAN, 70.3 podiums, BQ.

Cluster 06 · Clinics & podcasts

Talks, group sessions, interviews

Recap notes from clinics at clubs and gyms. Podcast appearances and our own conversations with researchers, coaches, and elite athletes. Slides & references included where possible.

What to expect

The Yousuli house style.

Practical > theoretical

  • If you can’t use it on Saturday, we don’t write it
  • Numbers, ranges, protocols, not vibes
  • Worked examples from real athletes

Plain language

  • Jargon defined the first time it appears
  • Diagrams where they earn their keep
  • Calculator links so you can plug in your own numbers

References when they matter

  • Peer-reviewed sources cited
  • Honest about strength of evidence
  • Case studies labelled as case studies, not "data"

No hot takes

  • If we change our minds, we say so
  • We’d rather be slow and right than fast and wrong
  • Cycling/running/swim equally welcome
