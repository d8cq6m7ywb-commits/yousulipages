How it’s built

Three formulas turn VO2max into a finish time.

01

Speed from VO2

v (km/min) = VO2max × fraction / Cr

Cr = oxygen cost per km. Lower Cr = better economy = faster at the same VO2.

02

Fraction by distance

The % of VO2max you can hold drops with distance. ~98% at 1 km, ~85% at 5 km, ~78% at half marathon, ~73% at marathon. Auto-set by the distance dropdown.

03

Power from speed

W/kg = speed (m/s) / RE

Running effectiveness (RE) translates speed to power. Default 1.05 m/s per W/kg is the well-trained adult range.

Coach note

Cr matters more than VO2max for most runners.

Two athletes with identical VO2max can run very different times: a Cr of 180 vs 220 is a ~20% speed difference at the same fraction. Lab-measured Cr from a treadmill protocol is the cleanest input here. Plug measured Cr in and the predictions tighten dramatically.

If your fraction-plausibility flag goes red, your VO2max may be over-estimated, re-test, or reduce VO2max until the predicted time matches your honest race PR for that distance.
