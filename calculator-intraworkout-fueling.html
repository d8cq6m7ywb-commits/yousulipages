How the targets are built

Three modes, three philosophies — pick the one that matches your training history and goal.

01

Minimum — the floor

Conservative rates: 0–75 g/h depending on duration. Best for athletes new to fueling, sessions in heat, or anyone with a sensitive gut. Won’t maximize performance, but protects the day.

02

Recommended — default

Most athletes, most sessions. 10 g/h on short stuff, ramps to ~90 g/h on Ironman-length efforts. Aligns with current sports-nutrition consensus (ACSM, Burke et al.).

03

Maximum — race rate

120–150 g/h on the longest events. Only if you’ve trained your gut — weeks of progressive fueling sessions, mixed glucose+fructose source, practiced at race intensity. Don’t debut on race day.

The food-equivalent breakdowns assume average sizes — check your actual gel and drink labels (they vary 20–35 g/serving). The 1.8 : 1 maltodextrin to fructose ratio is the multi-transporter sweet spot for absorption rates above 60 g/h.

Coach note

Your gut is a trainable organ.

The single biggest reason athletes blow up at hour 3 of a 70.3 isn’t legs — it’s a stomach that hasn’t been trained to process 80–100 g/h. SGLT1 (glucose) and GLUT5 (fructose) transporters upregulate with repeated exposure. Translation: practice your race fueling in training, not race week.

A simple build:

  • Weeks 1–2: 40–50 g/h on long rides — comfortable, build the habit
  • Weeks 3–5: 60–70 g/h, introduce mixed glucose+fructose source
  • Weeks 6+: 80–100+ g/h, dial in your specific products at race intensity
