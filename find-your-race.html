The idea

Not every race suits every athlete. The course chooses winners as much as the training does.

The right race is a performance decision, not a travel decision

Two courses at the same distance can ask completely different things of you. A flat, still-water, fast course rewards a big sustainable engine and a sharp aero position. A hilly, hot, technical course rewards durability, heat tolerance, and the ability to surge and recover. Put the same athlete on both and the finish times, and the places, can be worlds apart.

Most athletes pick a race for the destination, the date, or because a training partner is doing it. Then they train for a year and find out on race morning that the course never suited them. Choosing the course that fits your physiology is one of the highest-leverage decisions in your whole season, and it happens before you run a single session.

That is what this is. I match you to your course using data, then we build the plan around the choice.

Two questions, two answers

Depending on your goal, you usually want one of these, sometimes both.

Question one

Where do I go fastest?

The course that flatters your strengths and hides your weaknesses, for an outright personal best. If the goal is a time, a finish, or a story to tell, this is the one. We find the profile of course your numbers are built to attack.

Question two

Where can I podium or qualify?

A fast time and a slot are not the same target. A slot depends on your age group, the depth of the field, the gender pool, and how the course thins that field. Sometimes the smart move is a slower course where your relative strengths matter more and the fast specialists stay home.

Why the two answers can differ

Your fastest course and your best slot course are often not the same race. A pancake-flat, competitive event might be where you clock a PB, while a brutal, hot, hilly one might be where your durability drags you onto the podium as the fast-but-fragile athletes fall apart. Knowing the difference, and choosing on purpose, is the whole point.

How it works

Three inputs, one recommendation you can act on.

1

Your profile

A questionnaire on your goals, history, life, and constraints, plus your training data and any Performance Lab numbers you have (VO2max, thresholds, aero, heat and sweat, critical power). Together these describe what kind of athlete you actually are, not what you wish you were.

2

The course data

I hold performance data on every IRONMAN and 70.3 course: how they ride and run, how times spread, what the fields and conditions look like. That is the reference I read your profile against.

3

Your shortlist

A ranked short list of races with the reasoning behind each: which fit you for a personal best, which fit you for a slot, and what each one will ask of you. Then, if you are coached, we build the season around the target.

Coach note

This is not a spreadsheet of slot counts you could look up yourself. Plenty of good analysts will tell you where the slots are. What I add is you: your physiology, read against the course, so the recommendation is about which race your body is built for, not just which race hands out the most tickets.

What this is, and what it is not

I would rather set the expectation straight than oversell it.

Not a guarantee

No analysis can promise you a slot or a podium. Fields change, conditions change, and on the day you still have to race well. What this does is stack the odds in your favour by putting you on a course that suits you, instead of leaving the choice to chance.

Only as good as the inputs

The more I know about you, the sharper the recommendation. Real lab numbers beat estimates, and honest answers on the questionnaire beat flattering ones. If you have never tested, we can still do good work from training data, it is just a slightly wider aim.

A decision tool, not a booking service

I help you choose the race and understand why. Entering it, travel, and logistics are yours to arrange. If you are coached, the choice flows straight into your plan.

No, but it helps. With VO2max, aero, heat, or critical-power data the match is sharper. Without it, we work from your training data and race history, which is still plenty to point you in the right direction.

The course database is built out fullest for IRONMAN and 70.3, so those are where the analysis is strongest. If you are targeting other events, get in touch and I will tell you honestly how much I can add.

How is this different from the slot-counting analysts out there?

They tell you where the slots are, which is genuinely useful. I read the course against your physiology, so the answer is about which race you are built for, not just which one hands out the most tickets. Different question, and for most athletes the more useful one.
