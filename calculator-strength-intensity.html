How it works

One formula for 1RM, two models for prescription.

01

Epley 1RM

1RM = weight × (1 + 0.0333 × reps)

Most accurate at 1–12 reps (the ACE / NSCA validity band). We accept 1–20 to cover endurance-strength work, but flag a warning chip past 12, the estimate typically over-predicts by 5–15% in higher-rep sets. Past 20 reps the estimate is meaningless.

02

NSCA fixed bands

The classical NSCA recommendation: training phase determines load %. General/early prep 60–70% · Hypertrophy 70–80% · Late prep + Competition 80–90% · Transition flexible. Same prescription every session.

03

Auto-regulated (RPE)

Reads how you feel today. Last warm-up RPE ≤ 7 → +5% on the day’s load (you’re fresh). RPE ≥ 9 → −5% (cooked, preserve quality). RPE 8 → keep the standard band. Smarter for in-season blocks.

Phases of training

Five phases. Use the one that matches where you are in your annual plan.

Off-season

General / early specific

Foundation strength, movement patterns. 2–3 sets · 12+ reps · <30 s rest · 60–70% 1RM. Build the base before chasing numbers.

Build

Hypertrophy

Muscle mass, work capacity. 3–6 sets · 6–12 reps · 30–90 s rest · 70–80% 1RM. Most endurance-athlete strength blocks live here.

Pre-season

Late specific

Sport-specific strength. 2–6 sets · ≤6 reps · 2–5 min rest · 80–90% 1RM. Heavier loads, fewer reps, longer rests.

Race

Competition

Peak strength + power. 3 sets · ≤6 reps · 2–5 min rest · 80–90% 1RM. Minimal volume, maximal output. Don’t accumulate fatigue.

Recovery

Transition

Active recovery, maintenance, movement quality. 1–2 sets · 8–15 reps · 30–90 s rest · flexible load. Keep moving without accumulating cost.

Tempo & movement speed

Tempo controls the stimulus, same load, different result.

Tempo notation has 4 numbers: Eccentric – Pause – Concentric – Pause (in seconds). X means “as fast as possible.”

  • 3-1-1-0, 3 s lowering, 1 s pause at the bottom, 1 s up, no pause at the top. Hypertrophy bias.
  • 2-0-2-0, even, controlled. General strength / hypertrophy.
  • 2-1-1-1, deliberate, slight pauses. Max strength.
  • 2-0-X-1, controlled lowering, explosive push, brief reset. Power / speed.

Power, simplified: P = (mass × distance) / time. A 60 kg bar moved 0.6 m in 1.2 s vs. 0.6 s, the faster rep produces roughly double the power. Same load, same range. Speed of intent is why athletes train explosive concentrics even at sub-maximal loads.

Coach note

Strength is a small input that returns big interest.

Most endurance athletes under-strength-train and over-cardio. Two 30-minute sessions a week, year-round, returns more than the third easy run you cut to fit them in. The gains compound: better economy, fewer injuries, more durable late-race watts.

  • Start with patterns, not numbers, goblet squat, RDL, push-up, row, plank. Movement quality before load.
  • 2–3 weeks of general/early prep first, then progress to hypertrophy. Don’t skip the on-ramp.
  • Lift heavy in the off-season, lift to maintain in-season. The race-week strength session is 1 set × 5 reps × 80–85%, not failure work.
  • Auto-regulate when training load is high, the RPE model exists because hard endurance days fatigue you for hard strength days.
