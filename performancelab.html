Run / Gait Analysis

$100Remote review
$150Trackside tune-up
$300Pro biomech + metabolic

Improve how you move, reduce injury risk, and convert more force into forward motion. Three depths: a remote video review, a 30-min trackside session, or a 60-min biomech deep dive paired with VO₂ and SmO₂ data.

What you get

  • Remote ($100): two clips (side + rear), 48 h annotated review, red-flag report, drill pack for hip stability, cadence, and foot strike
  • Trackside ($150): 30-min filmed session, freeze-frame form tweaks, static + dynamic mobility screen, personalized strength circuit and cadence targets
  • Pro ($300): 60-min deep dive — HD video + foot-mounted motion sensors + treadmill VO₂ + SmO₂ monitoring; running economy, effectiveness, left-right oxygen balance

Running economy vs. effectiveness (coach note)

  • Economy: your “miles per gallon” — less oxygen burned at a given pace = stay fresh longer
  • Effectiveness: how much of your leg power actually pushes you forward (vs. bouncing up or side-to-side)

Ideal for: runners battling recurring injuries, triathletes whose run leg falls apart late, masters athletes wanting to stay durable, and serious racers chasing margin.

Most athletes feel lighter on their feet within a few sessions. Meaningful efficiency gains and injury-risk reduction show up over 8–12 weeks of consistent drill work.

Your VO₂max is a direct window into how efficiently your body transports and uses oxygen at peak effort. Field tests and wearables estimate. A graded ramp with breath-by-breath gas exchange measures — and gives you zones built from your own data.

Why it matters

  • Benchmark. Retest every 8–12 weeks to quantify gains and spot plateaus before the season slips

What’s included

Pre-test

Physiology · Metabolic & fueling

Metabolic Assessment (RMR + Body Composition)

$120RMR + body comp

Generic formulas can miss your true Resting Metabolic Rate by 15–25%. We measure it directly with medical-grade gas analysis — so calorie targets, fueling, and recovery decisions sit on actual data.

Why it matters

  • Precision over estimates. Real RMR replaces formula-based guesses
  • Early RED-S warning. Two-thirds of endurance athletes drift into low energy availability; a suppressed RMR is one of the first physiological flags — before bone-stress injuries, hormonal disruption, and stalled progress
  • Body-comp clarity. Calipers + scale to ~1% precision — track changes that aren’t just bodyweight

What’s included

  • Indirect-calorimetry RMR test (~15 min, relaxed breathing)
  • Body composition profile (lean mass, fat mass, % change potential)
  • Personalized report within 48–72 h: measured vs. predicted RMR, daily calorie & macro ranges (rest / training / heavy days), 5 example meal templates
  • Optional 20-min video call to interpret results

How to prep

  • Best in the morning · fasted: no food, caffeine, or alcohol for 5 h · no smoking 2 h prior
  • No hard workouts for 12 h · light mobility is fine · comfortable clothing (no compression)

Ideal for: endurance athletes fine-tuning fueling, strength athletes chasing lean mass without under-eating, anyone rebounding from injury or over-training, masters athletes tracking age-related metabolic change.

If results flag elevated RED-S risk, we outline referral options (sports dietitian, blood work) and a re-test timeline.

Physiology · Surge & recovery

Performance Recovery Profile (PRP)

$329Full PRP

Sprint, recover, repeat — that’s the coin of the realm in HYROX, CrossFit, criteriums, and short-course triathlon. The PRP measures how hard you can surge and how fast you bounce back, so you pace attacks and intervals on data, not feel.

Why it matters

  • Surge power wins races. A 5% gap in recovery speed can mean 1st vs. 5th when the final interval hits
  • Limiter detection. VO₂ + lactate + SmO₂ trends show whether the bottleneck is aerobic refill, buffering capacity, or local muscle perfusion
  • Targeted training. Knowing the culprit lets you choose tempo float intervals, glycolytic sprints, or active-recovery tweaks — instead of generic threshold work

Test protocol

  • Warm-up + 3–4 all-out 30–60 s bouts on treadmill, rower, or bike
  • Recovery windows (4–8 min) with continuous VO₂, SmO₂, and lactate finger-sticks
  • Repeat to confirm consistency — ~40 min total test time

Deliverables (within 72 h)

  • Peak power/pace vs. recovery curves (VO₂, lactate, SmO₂)
  • Time-to-recover metrics at 50% and 90% of baseline
  • Identified limiter (cardio-metabolic / buffering / local O₂)
  • Custom interval prescriptions and pacing advice

Ideal for: HYROX and CrossFit athletes hammering repeats, road racers and crit specialists, short-course triathletes, and tactical/team-sport athletes who live on burst-recovery patterns.

FAQ

How to choose a test, who these services are for, and what to expect from the process.

Which assessment should I start with?

Start with the assessment that matches your clearest bottleneck. Technique issues usually point to swim stroke, gait, or aero testing. Training-zone uncertainty points to VO₂max. Fueling questions point to RMR.

Do I need to be an advanced athlete?

No. Some services are advanced, but several entry points work well for age-group athletes who simply want clearer answers and smarter decisions. Beginners are explicitly welcome.

Can I combine services?

Yes. Common combos: VO₂max + RMR for full metabolic, or Swim Stroke + Bike CdA for technique-focused free speed. PRO Full-Stack Profiling bundles the deeper physiology stack.

Will I get actionable takeaways?

That’s the standard. Every test produces a one-page summary for the athlete (zones, pacing, fueling, priorities, limiter diagnosis) plus a deeper technical appendix for the coach.

How long does a session take?

VO₂max ~45 min test + ~15 min debrief. Swim, gait, and CdA are typically 60–90 min on-site. Full-stack profiling can take a half-day depending on the protocol.

Do you work with para-athletes?

Yes. Yousuli has a Supported Athletes program with reduced-rate coaching and assessment support for committed elite and para-athletes. Email us with your goals.
