When to reach for it

Three real moments where this saves you a calculator app and a unit-conversion screw-up.

01

Coach gave you a metric pace; race is in miles

Plug 4:30/km into the run section, hit Calculate, you get min/mile, m/s, km/h, mph, and finish times for 5K, 10K, half, and marathon at that pace. No mental math at the start line.

02

Wetsuit-legal? · What do I wear?

Type the water temp in the temperature section to see wetsuit rules. Same panel covers what to wear on the bike and run from heat-stress hot to balaclava-cold.

03

Mixing your own bottle

The concentration calculator tells you the % carb of a custom mix, under 4% is too dilute, over 8% will sit in your stomach. Your race-day fueling depends on getting this right.

Coach note

A converter is a translator, not a coach.

This tool is good at one thing: making the same number readable in any unit. It will not tell you whether 4:00/km is your pace today, whether 28 °C means you should change race-day fueling, or whether your 10% carb drink is the right call for a 70.3.

For those calls, you need a coach who knows you and a lab measurement that grounds the targets, threshold, FTP, fat-oxidation rate, sweat rate. Without that, you’re converting numbers in a vacuum.
