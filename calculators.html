Race & performance predictors

Estimate finish time from a single point, or work backward from your goal — the speed and pace you need to hit on race day.

Triathlon

Triathlon finish time

Pick a race distance, enter your paces and transition time. See cumulative splits, projected finish, and DQ-cutoff check. Useful for pacing the bike if you want a specific run window.

Open → Run

Running time predictor

Single-point estimator: enter one race result and project your finish time at any other distance. Or work backward from a target.

Open → Run · advanced

Advanced running predictor

Multi-point predictor: enter your best 400 m, 1,600 m, and 5,000 m and get a richer estimate of distance-specific finish times.

Recommended in conjunction with the single-point predictor Open → Bike

Power to finish

Enter race, bike, and rider data to see finish time, race-map preview, technical segments, climbs, descents, and hard turns.

Open → Run · qualifier

Boston qualifier calculator

Enter your age group to check exactly how fast you have to run to make it to Boston — without donating a gazillion dollars.

Open → Triathlon · qualifier

IM & IM 70.3 World Championship qualifier

Quick way to check if you’re competitive enough for IM Worlds and 70.3 Worlds — and what podium time looks like. Uses 2026 maths.

Fueling & metabolism

How much to eat in training, on race day, and at rest — backed by your actual numbers, not generic averages.

Workout fueling

Intra-workout fueling

Quick reminder of how much to consume during training. Enter duration and fueling mode (minimal / recommended / max) and see suggested intake.

Open → Race day

Triathlon race-day fueling

Enter pace and power strategy plus a host of athlete-specific data — print out a complete race-day fueling strategy.

Highly recommended for long events Open → Hydration

Sweat rate calculator

Type duration, weight before, weight after, and liquids consumed to get sweat rate and approximate sodium loss.

Open → Calorie burn

Triathlon calorie burn

Quick estimate of how many calories you’re burning during training across swim, bike, and run.

Open → Resting metabolism

RMR calculator

Different methods to estimate your resting metabolic rate — the energy your body needs at rest. Eating below RMR is a common path to performance and health issues.

Pair with the lab’s measured RMR for highest accuracy Open → Daily energy

Macro & TDEE

Build out your total daily energy expenditure including training, RMR, and lifestyle — with macro splits.

Open → Substrate use

RPE / RER estimate

A playful attempt to link Rate of Perceived Exertion to Respiratory Exchange Ratio — how much of your energy comes from fat vs. carbs.

Bike & aero

Free speed, smarter gearing, and pressure / drag estimates that actually reflect your setup.

Aero

CdA estimate

Enter data manually or load a FIT file to estimate your coefficient of aerodynamic drag (CdA).

Strongly recommended if you want to go faster at the same power Open → Rolling resistance

Crr calculator

Reverse calculator: if you know power, speed, and CdA, this gives or confirms your tire rolling resistance.

Best paired with aero-sensor data Open → Tires

Tire pressure guide

Improve safety, speed, and comfort with the correct pressure for road, mountain, recumbent, tandem, and more.

Open → Critical power

Critical power estimator

Go beyond FTP. Estimate your critical power using three maximal efforts to map both anaerobic and aerobic capacity.

Open → Drivetrain

Gear ratio table

How chainrings (front) and cassette cogs (rear) work together to turn your wheels a given number of times. Compact reference table.

Open → Drivetrain · advanced

Gear × speed × power

An advanced gear table that adds speed and power output. Good for checking gears for specific climbs and breakaways.

Swim

Critical swim speed and stroke-efficiency tests — the same tools we use poolside in coaching.

CSS

Critical swim speed

Enter two distances (100/400, 200/400, or whatever you have). Get CSS, finish-time projections at different lengths, and training zones.

Open → Stroke efficiency

EBT I — Efficiency Battery Test

Looks at height, stroke rate, and distance per stroke to tell you whether you should focus on increasing stroke rate or improving DPS.

Open → Stroke efficiency · advanced

EBT II — sets & tipping points

Looks at sets of 50 m / yard at varying stroke rates to highlight tipping points where efficiency plummets, and shows normal DPS / SR ranges at given speeds.

Run, strength & body

Pacing, plyometrics, strength load, and body composition.

Run zones

Running HR zones

Simple pacing strategies using heart-rate data — useful as a starting point or when you don’t have lab-derived zones.

Open → Plyometrics

Reactive Strength Index (RSI)

Measured with a simple jump test. Helps estimate how much bounce and free speed you can get in running.

Strength training and plyos are your friends Open → Strength training

Strength training intensity guide

How much to train during each phase of your plan, from early base to race week. Intensities, sets, rest intervals, plus exercise examples.

Open → Body composition

Calipers to body composition

Calculate body-fat percentage and lean body mass using calipers. Useful for ensuring weight loss isn’t muscle loss.

Data tools

Work with the actual files your devices produce — FIT-file analysis and multisport-leg splitting.

FIT file analysis

FIT file compare

Compare up to three FIT files from different devices. Visualise power, heart rate, cadence, and speed; check data quality before your next race.

Open → FIT file splitting

Multisport FIT breaker

Multisport FIT-file splitter for triathletes and cyclists. Upload, adjust leg boundaries, and export for WKO, TrainingPeaks, and others.

Open → Conversion

All-in-one conversions

Pace conversions, temperatures, concentrations, and more. Useful when traveling, when you want to convert finish time to pace, or vice versa.
