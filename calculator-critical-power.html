How critical power works

The model in three pieces.

01

Two-parameter model

P = W′ / t + CP

CP is the asymptote of the power-duration curve, the highest sustainable power. W′ is a finite work capacity above CP; spend it and you crack. Linear regression of P vs 1/t from your three efforts gives both numbers.

02

CP ≈ FTP, but cleaner

For most athletes CP and FTP are within 5%. CP’s advantage: built from multiple efforts at different durations, so it’s less sensitive to a bad 20-min effort or anaerobic outliers that wreck the 0.95× shortcut. Better signal-to-noise.

03

W′ tells you race tactics

Big W′ (25+ kJ) = sprinter / puncheur. Small W′ (15 kJ) = diesel / steady-state engine. Same CP, totally different way to race. Drives breakaway timing, finish-line tactics, and the kind of power profile to develop.

Coach note

Three things that wreck a CP test, and how to fix them.

  • Pacing. Hard out from gun, hold to failure. If you sandbag the start to "pace", the effort isn’t maximal and CP comes out low. Negative-split CP tests are useless.
  • Spacing. Three efforts at different durations spread across the curve. Two efforts that are too close (e.g. 4 min + 5 min) make the regression unstable. Stick to ~2-3 / ~5-7 / ~12-15 min.
  • Recovery. Same day is OK with 30+ min between efforts, but fatigue from the first effort drags down the others. Better: spread across 2-3 days at the start of a training block when fresh.

The math also assumes constant-power steady efforts, not surges. Use a smart trainer or a flat road with no traffic. And keep your 5-min effort somewhere near 5 min, the calculator uses the closest-to-5-min effort to anchor the VO2max zone, so a 3-min "5-min" effort biases the top zone.

The metrics, plain English

What CP, FTP, AeT, and W′ are, and aren’t.

FTP (Coggan)

~60-min sustainable

Functional threshold power, the highest power you can sustain ~60 minutes. Field shortcut: 0.95×20-min mean max, not universal. Anaerobicly strong riders over-estimate; very aerobic riders under-estimate. CP is usually a cleaner number.

CP

The asymptote

Power-duration curve flattens to CP. Above CP, you spend W′. Below CP, in theory you can ride indefinitely. Practically, ~30-60 min hold time depending on glycogen, heat, and prior fatigue.

AeT / LT1

Top of "easy"

Aerobic threshold, the upper bound of comfortable aerobic work. Lactate near baseline; conversation easy. Roughly 60-76% of CP for most athletes, but varies with training history.

AnT / LT2 / MLSS

Highest steady state

Maximal lactate steady state, lactate levels off rather than climbing. Conceptually close to CP for most, but not identical. The line between "can hold" and "cracking soon".

W′

The reservoir above CP

Finite anaerobic work capacity in kJ. Spend it above CP, recover it below. Recovery rate depends on how far below CP you are, faster as you ride easier.
